Purpose

The main goal of this analysis to provide insight and prediction using effective visualization and machine learning with NOAA’s data sets.

What is Wildfire

A wildfire is an uncontrolled/unplanned fire in an area of combustible vegetation occurring in rural areas. Depending on the type of vegetation present, a wildfire can also be classified more specifically as a brush fire, bush fire, desert fire, forest fire, grass fire, hill fire, peat fire, vegetation fire or veld fire.1

Data Exploration

The data set has over 1.6 million of rows and 51 variables comprising of several weather events such as Tornado, Hail, Wildfire (rank # 25), Flooding, all within the properties of United States. Our goal is to deep dive into California Wildfire Events and see the following:

  • Wildfire by Months

  • Wildfire Property and Crop damages
  • Can we predict future occurrence
  • Is there a difference during Covid19 lockdown from March/June 2020 vs. the past.

## Length of Wildfire in Days

Length of wildfire in days is the difference of “Ending date of fire + 1 day” minus “Begining date of fire” and is measured in days. We added 1 because if the date is equal they will produce zero and would not be reflected in out data.
